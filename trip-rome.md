### Weather
Expect hot, dry conditions with temperatures averaging 88-90°F (31-32°C) during the day, occasionally reaching 104°F (40°C). Evenings cool to around 64°F (17°C). July is typically the hottest month with minimal rainfall (~20mm). The UV index peaks at 10—sunburn can occur in just 15 minutes without protection.

**Pro tip:** Rome has 2,500 *nasoni* (small drinking fountains) throughout the city with cold, clean water—carry a refillable bottle!

## Top Attractions

1. **The Colosseum** - The world's largest Roman amphitheater and one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World. Book tickets in advance to avoid 90-minute lines.

2. **Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel** - Home to Michelangelo's masterpiece ceiling and countless artistic treasures. Arrive before 9 AM to beat crowds.

3. **St. Peter's Basilica** - The world's largest church with stunning Renaissance architecture. Remember to cover shoulders and knees.

4. **Roman Forum & Palatine Hill** - The ancient political and commercial heart of Rome, adjacent to the Colosseum.

5. **The Pantheon** - A marvel of ancient engineering with its famous open oculus dome. Free entry.

6. **Trevi Fountain** - Baroque masterpiece best visited early morning (7-9 AM) or late evening (after 10 PM) to avoid crowds.

7. **Spanish Steps** - Iconic gathering spot connecting Piazza di Spagna to Trinità dei Monti church.

## Practical Tips

- **Visa**: US citizens do not need a visa for stays under 90 days
- **Dress code**: Cover shoulders and knees for churches (including St. Peter's)—you will be turned away otherwise
- **Tipping**: Service charge usually included; small tips (€1-2) appreciated but not expected
- **Transportation**: Walk or use public transit (metro/bus). Download ATAC Roma app. Avoid driving—traffic is chaotic
- **Dining**: Dinner service starts around 9 PM. Try *aperitivo* at 7 PM if hungry earlier
- **Free museums**: First Sunday of each month, state-owned museums are free
- **Cash**: Carry €100-150 in small bills for markets, tips, and small trattorias. Most places accept cards.
- **Useful phrases**: *Buongiorno* (Good morning), *Grazie* (Thank you), *Per favore* (Please), *Mi scusi* (Excuse me)
